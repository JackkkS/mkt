Robotics and Coding III (Older than 14 years old)


No.
Lesson Topic


Key Objective

1 It’s Your Future
- Students will learn about the need for problem solvers
- Students will demonstrate that they know the meanings of STEM, engineering, and robotics

2 Let’s Get Started I
- Students will be exposed to key resources that aid in working with the VEX IQ platform
- Students will learn about hardware, and Controller
3 Let’s Get Started II
- Students will pair their Controller with the Robot Brain
- Students will be able to recognize VEX IQ hardware parts and components visually

4 TALLEST TOWER I
- Create a tall tower to withstand a simulated earthquake
- Create an engineering notebook where their work can be organized
- Evaluate their designs to improve and enhance them

5 TALLEST TOWER II
- Analyze how a building’s design withstands earthquakes
- Apply building techniques and skills for strengthening
- Understand the design features that need to be considered when designing a tower

6 Your First Robot I
- Students will be able to follow instructions to build a functional VEX IQ robot
7 Your First Robot II
- Students will learn about and utilize the design process
- Students will be able to troubleshoot and solve problems to improve design
8 DRIVE FORWARD & REVERSE I
- Build an Autopilot robot and configure the Smart sensors
- Identify programming behaviors

9 DRIVE FORWARD & REVERSE II
- Program the Autopilot to drive forward and in reverse
- Explore drivetrain programming blocks and test student created programs
10 TURNING
- Students will explore the concepts of: Scientific investigation, Conversion and storage of energy investigating and evaluating variables
