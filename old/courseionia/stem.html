STEM Design Challenge (6-9 years old)


No.
Lesson Topic


Key Objective

1 Wind-up car
Students can make clocks and moving toys, wind-up mechanisms have long, coiled strips of springy material called mainsprings that store energy

2 Bottle raft
Students can make a raft to escape and simple matter of balancing forces and also the bowl of pebbles on the lollipop stick platform pushes the raft into water
3 Wind turbine
Students can explore the engineering challenge of extracting energy from the wind by building your own wind turbine, using paper cups to make the blades.

4 Tower crane
Students can explore the science and engineering behind these amazing machines by building their own working model crane.
5 Automation
Students can make a shark automation that swishes its tail and opens its jaws. These movements are controlled by devices called cams and cranks.

6 Blubber glove
Students can use the suit to work just like the fatty blubber that whale, dolphins, and seals have to protect them from cold.
7 Thermos
Students can use the suit to work just like the fatty blubber that whale, dolphins, and seals have to protect them from cold.

8 Pythagoras cup
Students can keep cold drinks cold for longer by carrying them in a thermos, A thermos reduces the transfer of heat, and so keeps hot drinks hot and cold drinks cold for longer.

9 Air freshener
Students can evaporate more slowly, so they can make your room smell great for weeks
10 Bubble tower
Students can make the reaction produces amazing bubbles that rise through a tower of oil and then fall back down.
11 Copper reactions
Students can make the reaction produces amazing bubbles that rise through a tower of oil and then fall back down.

12 Spaghetti tower
Students can make tower to stay standing, it must be strong enough not to buckle under its own weight, and it must have a stable base

13 Newspaper stool
Students can make a stool so strong that it can support their weight by using lots of sheets of newspaper together

14 Suspension bridge
Students can do the bridge by using bundles of drinking straws instead of concrete, and string instead of steel cables.

15 Geodesic dome
Students can make geodesic dome. It’s easy to make, and though it is quite light and looks fragile, it is actually extremely sturdy because of its shape

16 Pantograph
Students can make pantograph which is a device that was once used to copy drawings and enlarge them at the same time.

17 Wave machine
Students can use waves transfer energy to send and receive information, to heat up food, and to surf

18 Spectroscope
Students will study how to organize data in tables, graphs and study how to find the central tendency

19 Singing spoons
Students will study how to use metal spoons to create amazing noises that sound like bells or gongs chiming.

20 Harmonica
Students will study how to make some musical notes, and to learn a bit about the science of sound. Just like a real harmonica

21 Buzzer
Students will study how to use a simple rubber band to mimic a flying bee. The rubber band twists back and forth rapidly as you whirl it through the air

22 Guitar
Students will study how to make a surprisingly tuneful sound. In fact, students will find this project really hits the right notes
