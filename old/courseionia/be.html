Biomedical Engineering (Biomedical Engineering)


No.
Lesson Topic


Key Objective

1 Integumentary system I
Introduction about Biomedical engineering and 10 systems that they are going to learn. First, integumentary system students will learn
- What is skin?
- Function of the skin
- Part of the skin
- What has changed from the skin? One of the functions of the skin is to get different feelings, so its link to the temperature sensor of Kidbright.
2 Integumentary system II
Students will understand more about medical equipment of biomedical engineering and also new innovation. It’s been fun and challenging with the EV3 touch sensor.
3 Integumentary system III
Students will learn about the main substance that causes skin color (melanin). This lesson will design a mini project about skin reflection.

4 Musculoskeletal system, Skeleton system and Nervous system I
In this lesson student will learn
- How important are muscles and bones?
- Structure and function of muscles and bones
- Pathology of musculoskeletal
- How muscles and bones work? Students will get creative with making prosthetic fingers.

5 Musculoskeletal system, Skeleton system and Nervous system II
In this lesson students will learn function, structure and pathology of nerves and also know about reflex.
Students will understand more by reflex worksheet, reflex activity and reaction time test.

6 Musculoskeletal system, Skeleton system and Nervous system III
Combine 3 systems including muscles, bones and nerves together, so students will understand how it works together. Robotic arm (WeDo 2.0) is a good example of these systems.

7 Nervous system especially ears I
In this lesson students will learn structure, function, nerve and deformation of the ear.

8 Nervous system especially ears II
In this lesson students will learn innovations that solve ear problems and learn how to adapt technology with the ear. Students will use a microbit and issue voice commands.

9 Circulatory system and Respiratory system I
In this lesson students will learn the structure and function of the circulatory system, how important the circulatory system is and understand the circulatory pathway.
Students will do a worksheet for more understanding about the circulatory pathway and code comes out in the form of animation by using scratch.
10 Circulatory system and Respiratory system II
In this lesson students will learn respiratory pathway and function of respiratory system. Students will understand these 2 systems more by heart and lung laboratory.
11 Circulatory system and Respiratory system III
Students will understand the pathway of circulatory system and respiratory system and also when both systems combine together.
Students will see and understand more by conveyor activity.
12 Digestive system I
In this lesson students will learn digestive pathways and organs in this system. Also,
function of the digestive system. Introducing robots about sensors, how to code robots to pick up or grab something. (VEX IQ)

13 Digestive system II
Students will learn the substances that were used to test various substances in the body and
continue the robots from the previous class by using the robot to pick up substances. (VEX IQ)
14 Excretory system
In this lesson students will learn excretory pathways, organs in this system and function of the excretory system,
especially the kidney which is very important.
Students will understand more by kidney laboratory.
15 Reproductive system
Students will learn the difference between male and female reproductive organs, function and understand more by testis and ovary laboratory.
16 Genetic I
In this lesson students will learn how important genetics is, function and structure of genetics. Show database of bioinformatics by NCBI website.
17 Genetic I
Students will know the structure of DNA and new technology about genetics. Learn what gene testing is being used today.
18 Project
The purpose of this module is to let students solve problems and show them in the form of a project. They will show ideas from all the lessons learned.
